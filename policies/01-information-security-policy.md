# Information Security Policy

> Fictional. Meridian Pay teaching example. Not real advice.

Document owner: Head of Security (CISO)
Approved by: Chief Technology Officer
Version: 1.0
Review: once a year, or after any major change

## Purpose

This policy is the umbrella. It states, in one place, that Meridian Pay takes the protection of customer and company information seriously, and it names the other policies that put that intent into practice. Everything else in this folder sits underneath it.

## Scope

This policy applies to everyone who works for Meridian Pay, including employees, contractors, and temporary staff, and to every system that stores or handles Meridian data, whether Meridian runs it or a provider runs it on Meridian's behalf.

## Policy

1. **Information is protected according to its classification.** Meridian sorts information into Restricted, Confidential, Internal, and Public, and protects each level accordingly. See the [Data Classification Policy](04-data-classification-policy.md).
2. **Access is granted on need, and no more.** People and systems get the least access they need to do their job. See the [Access Control Policy](02-access-control-policy.md).
3. **Security is a shared responsibility, and leadership owns the risk.** The CISO owns the programme and reports risk to the CTO. Every member of staff is responsible for following these policies.
4. **Risks are identified, assessed, and treated on purpose.** Meridian maintains a risk register and decides deliberately whether to accept, reduce, avoid, or transfer each significant risk.
5. **Incidents are reported and handled.** Anyone who suspects a security problem must report it. See the [Incident Response Plan](05-incident-response-plan.md).
6. **Providers are held to these standards.** Third parties who handle Meridian data are assessed before use and held to obligations in their contracts.
7. **Compliance is verified, not assumed.** Controls are reviewed and tested. Meeting a rule on paper is not the same as the control working.

## Roles

- **Board and CTO**: set the risk appetite and hold the CISO accountable.
- **CISO**: owns this policy and the programme, reports risk, and approves exceptions.
- **Security engineers and the platform team**: implement and operate the technical controls.
- **Head of Risk and Compliance**: tracks obligations and evidence.
- **All staff**: follow the policies and report concerns.

## Exceptions

Any exception to these policies must be requested in writing, carry a business reason and an end date, and be approved by the CISO. Exceptions are recorded and reviewed.

## How this maps to NIST

This policy is the written heart of the Cybersecurity Framework **Govern** function. It sets roles, sets the risk appetite, and points to the specific rules that deliver the other five functions. If an auditor asked "where does your security programme start," this is the page you would hand them.
