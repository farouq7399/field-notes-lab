# Acceptable Use Policy

> Fictional. Meridian Pay teaching example. Not real advice.

Document owner: Head of Security (CISO)
Version: 1.0
Review: once a year, and signed by every new joiner

## Purpose

This is the policy staff actually read, because they have to sign it. It says, in plain terms, how Meridian's laptops, accounts, and data may and may not be used. It exists to protect both the company and the individual.

## Scope

All staff, contractors, and temporary workers, and any device used to access Meridian systems or data.

## Policy

**Do**

1. Use your Meridian laptop and accounts for Meridian work.
2. Lock your screen when you step away.
3. Keep your laptop updated. Do not turn off the security tools installed on it.
4. Store Meridian data only in approved systems (Microsoft 365 and the approved business tools).
5. Report anything that looks wrong, a strange email, a lost device, an odd login prompt, to the security team straight away. You will never be in trouble for reporting.

**Do not**

1. Do not share your password or your multi-factor device with anyone, including colleagues and IT.
2. Do not copy Restricted or Confidential data onto personal devices, personal email, or personal cloud storage.
3. Do not plug in unknown USB devices.
4. Do not install unapproved software on your Meridian laptop.
5. Do not try to get around security controls, even to be more productive. Ask for an exception instead.

## Phishing and email

Most attacks start with an email. If a message pressures you to act fast, to move money, to buy gift cards, or to log in through a link, slow down and verify through a second channel. When in doubt, forward it to the security team.

## Consequences

Breaking this policy can lead to loss of access and, for serious cases, disciplinary action. The point is not to punish, it is to keep customer money and data safe.

## How this maps to NIST

This policy supports **Protect** (Awareness and Training) and feeds **Detect** and **Respond**, because a workforce that knows how to spot and report a problem is one of the cheapest and most effective controls a small company has. Signed acceptance is also the evidence an assessor will ask for that awareness training actually reaches people.
