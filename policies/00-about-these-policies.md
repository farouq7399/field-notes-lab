# About these policies

> Every policy in this folder is fictional. They were written for Meridian Pay, a made-up bank, so you can see what real security policies look like and practise adapting them. They are teaching examples, not legal or security advice. Do not adopt them for a real organisation without proper review.

## Why policies come first

People new to security often think it starts with tools and firewalls. It starts with decisions written down. A policy is just a decision the organisation has agreed to and can point to later. In the Cybersecurity Framework, most of the Govern function is exactly this: deciding who is responsible, what the rules are, and how much risk you will accept, then writing it so it holds.

## How to read each one

Each policy here follows the same simple shape, because a shape you can predict is a shape people actually read:

- **Purpose**: one paragraph on why the policy exists.
- **Scope**: who and what it applies to.
- **Policy**: the actual rules, kept short.
- **Roles**: who does what.
- **How this maps to NIST**: the tie back to the framework, so you can see where a written rule becomes a framework outcome.

## How to practise with them

Read one, then try to answer: which Cybersecurity Framework outcomes does this policy support, and which system in the inventory does it protect. If you can answer that for all five, you understand more about how a security programme hangs together than most people who have never had to write one.
