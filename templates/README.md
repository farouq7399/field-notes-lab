# Templates: turn findings into a plan

These two workbooks are the working tools a security team lives in day to day. They are not tied to one episode. Once you have assessed a system and found gaps, this is where the gaps go to get fixed.

## What is in here

- **poam-tracker.xlsx**: the Plan of Action and Milestones. The master list of every known weakness, its severity, who owns the fix, and by when. Pre-filled with a few realistic Meridian items, plus blank rows to add your own.
- **gap-assessment.xlsx**: a higher-level view. Where you are, where you want to be, and how big the gap is, per area. Good for planning a roadmap and for talking to leadership.
- **control-assessment.xlsx**: the Assess step, the SP 800-53A way. For each control, how you checked it (Examine, Interview, Test) and whether it actually works (Satisfied or Other than satisfied). Every failure feeds the POA&M. This is the honest join between "we have controls" and "our controls work."

## How they connect to everything else

- The **CSF profile** (Episode 1) tells you where the big gaps are.
- The **assessment** (Episode 2, step 5) finds specific control weaknesses.
- Those weaknesses land in the **POA&M**, which tracks them to done.
- The **gap assessment** rolls it up so leadership can see the shape of the work.

That is the whole loop of a small security programme on four spreadsheets: know your posture, categorise your systems, pick your controls, and track the gaps to closure.

## The one idea to take away

A security programme is not a document you finish. It is a list of gaps you keep closing. The POA&M is where that truth lives. A team with an honest, current POA&M is doing better than a team with a beautiful policy and no idea what is actually broken.
